Origin of the Margarita

The origins of any cocktail can be slighlty ambigous, which also is true when it comes to the margarita. One of the strongest claims of inventing the margarita was made by Francisco "Pancho" Morales, in 1942. He worked in a bar named Tommy's Place in Ciudad Juarez in Mexico and allegedly invented the cocktail when a guest came in asking for a "Magnolia".

Recipe

The making of a margarita is a balancing act. The key is to balance the sourness from the limes with the spiciness of the tequila and soften it out with sugar. Dont worry, here is your recipe for success!

Ingredients: Other supplies:
3 cl tequila Cocktail shaker
3 cl cointreau/triple sec Citrus press
2 cl simple syrup Ice
1 1/2 - 2 limes
1 tablespoon salt

Directions:

  1. Squeeze your limes into the cocktail shaker
  2. Measure up the tequila, pour it in shaker
  3. Measure up the cointreau, pour it in the shaker
  4. Measure up your simple syrup, pour it in shaker
  5. Put a generous amount of ice into the shaker, close it up, and shake for 30 seconds
  6. Wet the edge of a glass with lime. Put salt on a plate, and rim the glass with a fine edge of salt
  7. Pour into the glass from the shaker. Make sure that the ice doesn't come with, strain with the lid of shaker or with any strainer you might have at home.
  8. Enjoy!
